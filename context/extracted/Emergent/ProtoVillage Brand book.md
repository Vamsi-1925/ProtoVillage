## 2. Logo system

### 2.1 Construction principle [R3 p4]

The original Graamam wordmark used a **customised typeface**. It "expressed the brand's story well, but was not repeatable across the family." **Caros Soft** was identified as a near match — "Only slight variation in G; rest of the forms match closely." Graamam therefore becomes the parent identity, and **all** brand logos adopt Caros Soft as the main font, with tagline weight varied per brand.

Two R3 slides give type specs and they differ in places — the unified-typography slide [R3 p5] and the final logo-application slides [R3 p10–p12]. Both are reproduced; where they conflict, p10–p12 (the final applied lockups) take precedence over p5 (the earlier system proposal).

### 2.3 What the marks actually look like

Descriptions are read from the logo artwork itself [R3 p2, p3, p5, p6, p10–p15], not from text.

- **Protovillage** — a circular **concentric-ring mark** resembling tree rings / a fingerprint / labyrinth in teal, paired with the wordmark **"Protovillage" in a maroon (dark red) serif**. Unchanged in the harmonization [R3 p10, p15].
- **Graamam** — the **zygote mark**: a teal ring (a cell) enclosing a smaller off-centre nucleus dot — a circle within a circle. Wordmark **"graamam"** set so that **"graam" is bold teal** and **"am" is lighter/regular teal**, with the descriptor **"handmade happiness"** beneath. A **dual-tone / reversed lockup** exists: on a solid dark-teal panel the ring and wordmark render in **pale cream** with a **gold ring** outline [R3 p6, p10].
- **Book-A-Loom** — a woven **loom / two-figures** mark: stylised human figures (dot heads) whose arms interlock into a repeated **X weave** (three chevron "X"s like crossed threads), rendered in a **teal gradient** (dark to lighter teal). Uppercase **"BOOK-A-LOOM"** wordmark over **"Your Gateway to Handcrafted Textiles"** [R3 p3, p10, p14].
- **Tuuriya** — *old:* a **tan hatched circular badge** with a **crescent moon over stylised water/wave lines**, "Tuuriya" + "Handcrafted Bliss." *New:* a **radial sunburst / dandelion mark** — fine gold rays inside a dotted ring with a small **maroon centre dot** — beside a **"tuuriya"** wordmark and **"Pure Awearness"** (the letters "wear" emphasised). A **teal-and-gold dual-tone sunburst** variant also appears [R3 p3, p12, p14].
- **Kaanthi** — a **ginkgo-leaf** illustration (fan-shaped, radiating veins). *Old:* gold line-art leaf + gold script "kaanthi." *New:* a **filled teal-and-gold ginkgo leaf** with the **"kaanthi"** wordmark set in the Samarkan (Devanagari-styled Latin) face, over **"illuminating the beautiful."** Described in-source as a **Dual-Tone Variation** [R3 p3, p11, p14].
- **5 Minute Gourmet** — a **stopwatch** mark integrated into the wordmark: the numeral **"5" doubles as the stopwatch dial**, with the stopwatch crown/button on top and **speed-lines** trailing to the right; "minute" set small and italic, and the stopwatch stands in for the "G" of **"Gourmet!"** (italic). Presented in several **dual-tone colourways** — teal/maroon, teal/gold, all-teal, and maroon/gold [R3 p3, p11, p13].

### 2.4 Legacy "by graamam" lockups [R3 p14]

A "New Legacy Logo" line endorses each child brand with a **"by graamam"** signature set in Caros Soft beneath the brand mark. Provided for **Book-A-Loom, 5 Minute Gourmet, Kaanthi, and Tuuriya** — each shows its new mark with **"by graamam"** as the endorsement descriptor (replacing or supplementing the brand's own tagline).

## 3. Typography

### 3.1 Logo / family font

**Caros Soft** is the family typeface for all logos, in varied weights: Bold, Medium, Regular, Light, and Caros (Medium) Italic. Kaanthi is the exception — its wordmark retains **Samarkan Normal** for Ayurvedic character, with a Caros Soft Light tagline [R3 p4, p5, p11].

### 3.2 Website & packaging fonts — three options [R3 p16]

The source presents **three complete options** and does **not** silently pick one on p16. The final slide [R3 p17] explicitly recommends **Option 1**.

**Option 1 — Modern Humanist  ✅ RECOMMENDED [R3 p17]**
- Website — Headings: **Raleway**; Body: **Nunito Sans**
- Packaging — Brand Name: **Caros Soft**; Storytelling: **Cormorant Garamond**; Product Information: **Avenir Next**
- Positioning: "Balanced and versatile. Creates a modern yet approachable web presence, while packaging carries an artisanal and premium tone."

**Option 2 — Premium Editorial**
- Website — Headings: **Playfair Display**; Body: **Inter**
- Packaging — Brand Name: **Caros Soft**; Storytelling: **GT Sectra**; Product Information: **Helvetica Neue**
- Positioning: "Premium and aspirational. A high-end editorial look online, with packaging that signals luxury and boutique positioning."

**Option 3 — Contemporary Craft**
- Website — Headings & Body: **Sofia Pro**
- Packaging — Brand Name: **Caros Soft**; Storytelling: **Tiempos Text**; Product Information: **Gill Sans**
- Positioning: "Consistent and modern. A unified digital [look]." *(source sentence is cut off on p16)*

## 5. Positioning, values, personas, tone, photography

All of §5 comes from the **older Graamam brand book** [Graamam book]. R3 is silent on every item here, so it is carried forward unchanged. It is written for **Graamam specifically**; treat it as the family's inherited narrative until a Proto-level version exists.

### 5.1 What Graamam is

**Graamam®** — acronym for **GRAAMeena Aarthika Mandali** (Rural Economic Zone), an initiative of ProtoVillage. Tagline: **Handmade Happiness**.

A bioregional rural economic system designed to augment the entrepreneurial spirit of rural women and transform their villages into communities that are **Ecologically Sustainable, Socially Cohesive, Economically Robust**. "Not an enterprise, but a rural economic model designed to cause happy communities and healthy ecosystems."

### 5.2 Why we exist — Foundation of Sustainable Societies

1. **Healthy Ecosystems** — make life possible
2. **Happy Communities** — make life worth living
3. **Sustainable Societies** — invest in nourishing what makes life possible and worth living

### 5.3 Positioning & FLOW

Graamam® is "the answer to our quest to design a rural economic system that primarily aims to cause happy communities and healthy ecological systems." Core device:

**FLOW = Fast to experience. Slow to make.**
- **FAST** — toxin-free, guilt-free living, now, on the go.
- **SLOW** — naturally grown, biodiverse, indigenous wisdom, traditional recipes, completely handmade.

### 5.4 Brand promise

Every product made from renewable natural resources by well-trained rural women; locally sourced, naturally grown, handmade; no harmful chemicals or preservatives; ecologically sustainable via circular local supply chains.

### 5.5 Brand values

- **Planet Positive** — products and by-products don't harm air, water, soil.
- **People Positive** — interdependent, resilient communities working together.
- **Purpose before and beyond profit** — evolving to nourish local ecosystems, led by communities.

### 5.6 Archetypes (Jung wheel)

- **Creator** — making space for innovation in products.
- **Sage** — traditional knowledge for the current world.
- **Citizen** — connecting people into a shared sense of belonging.

### 5.7 Personas (B2C)

- **Green Goddess Greeshma** — eco-evangelist; "what's good for the planet is good for me"; Environment ≫ Efficiency. Needs non-toxic, planet-friendly alternatives and proof of **no greenwashing**.
- **Conscious Consumer Karen** — event planner and mother; weighs certifications, justice, equity. Needs quality products made the right way by fairly-treated people; proof of **quality**.
- **Fitness Junkie Firoze** — trainer; macro measurer. Needs products that fit a planned diet, quick to use; proof of **ingredients**.

Segments: **B2B & B2C** [Graamam book]. *(See §1 conflict on Graamam's audience label in R3 p2.)*

### 5.8 Verbal tone — **Witty yet Wise**

| Trait | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Warm and Approachable | Friendly, simple | "We are happy to be second — the second best to the world's greatest chef — your ajji!" |
| Inspiring Action without Guilt | Optimistic, resilient | "A cheat meal turned into a cheat week? Get up, be kind to your body. Here's a starter kit…" |
| Genuine and Transparent | Open, thoughtful | "We make our products with love; love for you but importantly, love for the planet and the people who make them." |

Sample campaign copy ("The (NRI) heart wants what it wants" — Spicy Sesame Podi) is preserved in the source [Graamam book].

### 5.9 Photography / videography — "Visuals need to elicit a feeling"

Highlight product textures; use natural light with soft warm shadows; keep printed text sharp and readable; use occasion-relevant props; avoid highly textured backgrounds; always include a human element; product videos slow, deliberate, luxurious; no jarring animations or switches.

## 6. Contrast & accessibility (computed)

WCAG 2.x contrast ratios, computed (not estimated) for every measurable anchor and extended colour, against **white #FFFFFF** and against the palette's dark neutral **Charcoal #1D1D1B** (named the Core Neutral for typography in §4.2). Thresholds: **AA body text = 4.5:1**, **large text / UI shapes = 3:1**.

| Colour | Hex | vs White | vs #1D1D1B | Safe **body** text | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Anchor Teal | #1E6A72 | **6.25** | 2.70 | **White** | Dark text fails even at 3:1. White text only. |
| Anchor Secondary teal | #3595C | — | — | **UNMEASURABLE** | ⚠️ Malformed 5-digit hex. Cannot be built or rated until corrected. |
| Anchor Gold | #DDA444 | 2.22 | **7.61** | **Dark (#1D1D1B)** | ⚠️ Fails white at body **and** at 3:1 UI. Never put white text on it. |

| Dusty Rose | #D8A39D | 2.18 | **7.75** | **Dark (#1D1D1B)** | ⚠️ Fails white at body **and** at 3:1 UI. Never white text. |

### 6.1 Flags — colours that break as button fills with white text

A build tool will default to **white text on a coloured fill**. These fail that and will produce unreadable UI:

- **#DDA444 gold** — 2.22:1 with white. Fails body *and* the 3:1 large/UI floor. **Do not use as a white-text button.** Use dark #1D1D1B text (7.61:1), or reserve gold for surfaces/accents.
- **#D8A39D dusty rose** — 2.18:1 with white. Same failure. Dark text only (7.75:1).
- **#FDFAE4 cream** and **#D9C8A2 warm sand** — 1.05 and 1.65 with white. **Surfaces only, never text-bearing fills with white type.** Dark text only.
- **#A6654E clay (4.57)** and **#6B7A41 olive (4.68)** — clear 4.5:1 with white only barely. Safe for white-text buttons but with almost no margin; don't lighten them.

Every measurable colour *can* carry body text on **one** side (white or dark) — none is unusable outright — but the four above must be pinned to the correct text colour in any component library, and gold/rose/cream/sand must be blocked from taking white text automatically.
